Case Study 01: The 8th St. Project

The Analysis

Timeline & Budget

This project took significantly longer than anticipated due to a massive expansion of scope—moving from simple repairs to full ceiling replacements. While the client received premium services at a fraction of market cost, the "hurry" to finish led to the project's final friction point.

Limiting Factors

  • Unregulated third-party site access
  • Lockout/Key management failure
  • Pricing that left zero margin for accidental setbacks

Lessons Learned

"Proper Preparation is Prevention."

I learned that construction is a 'cut-throat' industry. If you don’t have a bulletproof system for site security and prep, the small things can overshadow the massive hard work you put in.

A Message to Marlin Satre

"In summary, I learned that the construction business is (in Marlin's words) cut-throat. It is very competitive and if you don't know what you're doing, you will get chewed up and spit out. I came back fighting every time and will continue to do so."
